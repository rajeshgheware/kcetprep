The rule: you already have the content. The next 4 days are won by active recall + spaced repetition, not by reading more. Every drill below assumes the formula/reaction is covered with your hand. Recite aloud. Check. Tick. Move on.
Target: ~12 exposures per card over 4 days. Each exposure is ≤10 seconds. Total time budget = ~20 min/day for drill (separate from study blocks in the 7-day tracker).

Technique — match the drill to the card type

① Named-reaction cards

Sandmeyer, Etard, Stephen, Rosenmund, Wurtz, Aldol, Cannizzaro, Hinsberg, Kolbe…

Method: on paper, draw reagent → arrow → product. 3× each. Then write one real example with an actual compound.

Toluene + CrO₂Cl₂/CS₂ → chromium complex → H₃O⁺ → PhCHO (= Etard)

② Formula cards

Nernst, Arrhenius, Kohlrausch, LCR Z, Bohr rn, SHM energy, ΔTb, de Broglie…

Method: write formula from memory. Then plug in one dummy numeric. Formula → numeric binds it.

Nernst: E = E° − (0.0591/n)log Q
At n=2, Q=10 → correction = −0.0296 V

③ Fact / statement cards

Lanthanoid contraction, diamagnetic χ < 0, d⁰/d¹⁰ colourless, amine basicity order, nuclear-force range…

Method: build a mnemonic (see below) OR a compare-table. Recite aloud 5×. No drawing needed.

"Glue-1 · Salt-2 · Potash-3 · Alum-5" → van't Hoff i for glucose/NaCl/K₂SO₄/Al₂(SO₄)₃

Mnemonics worth stealing

SBG diazonium — Sandmeyer (CuCl / CuBr / CuCN) · Balz-Schiemann (BF₄⁻, heat → ArF) · Gatterman (Cu/HX, cheaper)
ESR aldehyde prep — Etard (CrO₂Cl₂ / CS₂) · Stephen (RCN + SnCl₂/HCl) · Rosenmund (RCOCl + H₂/Pd-BaSO₄ poisoned)
van't Hoff i — "Glue-1, Salt-2, Potash-3, Alum-5" (glucose · NaCl · K₂SO₄ · Al₂(SO₄)₃)
Lanthanoid odd OS — "Ce 4, Eu 2, Tb 4, Yb 2" (4f⁰ / 4f⁷ / 4f⁷ / 4f¹⁴ stability)
KCET has NO negative marking — attempt all 60 Qs. Blank = 0 = wrong. Never leave a bubble empty.
Physics "30-sec rule" — if you can't see the approach in 30 sec, circle and move on. Come back at the end. Time is the enemy, not the Q.

Don't do any of these

  • Don't re-read passively. If your eyes aren't covered by your hand, you're familiarising, not memorising — different skill, different result under exam pressure.
  • Don't silent-read. Recite aloud. Auditory channel + verbal channel = 2× retention.
  • Don't add new chapters after Tue 21 Apr evening. Last 48 h = pure recall on what's already in.
  • Don't skip the applied test. 5 KCET MCQs after each evening drill. Formula without application falls out under pressure.
  • Don't pull an all-nighter Wed 22 Apr. Sleep consolidates memory. 10 PM lights-out is non-negotiable.
  • Don't discuss answers between Physics (11:50 AM) and Chemistry (2:30 PM). Confidence protection mode — whatever happened in Physics is done.
If you miss a slot, don't double up later. Just do the next scheduled slot. The cadence matters more than any single exposure. Tick the boxes honestly — the log is for you, not me.
