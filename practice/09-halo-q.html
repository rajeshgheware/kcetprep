Haloalkanes & Haloarenes — 10 MCQs

Target time: 13 min · Volatile chapter (11 Q across 2022–2025)
Key reactions:
  • SN1 order: 3° > 2° > 1° (carbocation stability); SN2: 1° > 2° > 3° (steric); polar protic favours SN1, polar aprotic favours SN2
  • Wurtz: 2 R-X + 2 Na / dry ether → R-R; Fittig: aryl–aryl; Wurtz-Fittig: aryl–alkyl
  • Finkelstein: R-Cl (or R-Br) + NaI / dry acetone → R-I; Swarts: R-Br + AgF → R-F
  • From diazonium: Sandmeyer (CuCl/HCl, CuBr/HBr, CuCN/KCN), Gattermann (Cu/HCl), replaces −N₂⁺ by −Cl/−Br/−CN

Q1. The correct order of reactivity towards SN2 reaction is:

  1. CH₃X > 1° > 2° > 3°
  2. 3° > 2° > 1° > CH₃X
  3. CH₃X > 3° > 1° > 2°
  4. All equal

Q2. The Finkelstein reaction involves:

  1. R-Cl + NaI → R-I + NaCl (in dry acetone)
  2. R-Cl + AgF → R-F + AgCl
  3. R-Br + Mg → R-MgBr
  4. R-X + KOH (alc) → alkene

Q3. The Swarts reaction converts:

  1. R-Cl → R-I
  2. R-Br / R-Cl → R-F using AgF, Hg₂F₂ or SbF₃
  3. R-X → R-OH
  4. R-X → R-H

Q4. Chlorobenzene reacting with sodium in dry ether gives:

  1. Toluene
  2. Biphenyl (Fittig reaction)
  3. Benzoic acid
  4. Phenol

Q5. A benzene diazonium salt on treatment with CuCl/HCl gives:

  1. Chlorobenzene (Sandmeyer)
  2. Phenol
  3. Bromobenzene
  4. Benzonitrile

Q6. In polar aprotic solvents (e.g., DMF, DMSO), SN2 reactions are:

  1. Accelerated because nucleophile is not solvated
  2. Slowed because nucleophile is strongly solvated
  3. Unaffected
  4. Converted to SN1

Q7. The most stable carbocation among the following is:

  1. CH₃⁺
  2. CH₃CH₂⁺
  3. (CH₃)₂CH⁺
  4. (CH₃)₃C⁺

Q8. Chlorobenzene does not undergo nucleophilic substitution under mild conditions because:

  1. C–Cl bond is strong due to partial double-bond character from resonance
  2. Cl is electron donating
  3. It is sterically hindered
  4. No lone pair on Cl

Q9. Dehydrohalogenation of 2-bromo-2-methylbutane with alc KOH gives the major product as:

  1. 2-methyl-1-butene
  2. 2-methyl-2-butene (more substituted alkene — Zaitsev)
