Q1. Order vs molecularity.

Answer: (B) Order experimental; molecularity theoretical whole number

Order can be 0, fractional or integer — from experiment. Molecularity is the number of particles in an elementary step, always a positive whole number.

Q2. k for 1st order, t1/2 = 40 min.

Answer: (A) 0.0173 min⁻¹

k = 0.693/t1/2 = 0.693/40 = 0.01733 min⁻¹.

Q3. Rate doubles when [A] doubles.

Answer: (B) 1

Rate ∝ [A]n. 2n = 2 ⇒ n = 1.

Q4. Pseudo first-order for ester hydrolysis.

Answer: (B) Water is in large excess, its [ ] essentially constant

True rate = k[ester][H₂O]. Since [H₂O] ≈ const, rate = k'[ester] → behaves first order.

Q5. Units of k for 2nd order.

Answer: (C) L mol⁻¹ s⁻¹

Rate (mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹) = k × [A]². k = (mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹)/(mol L⁻¹)² = L mol⁻¹ s⁻¹.

Q6. Role of catalyst.

Answer: (B) Provides alternate path with lower Ea

Catalyst does not alter ΔH or the position of equilibrium; it only lowers Ea so rate increases (for both forward and reverse).

Q7. 75% reacted in 60 min (1st order).

Answer: (C) 30 min

75% consumed = 2 half-lives (50% then 25% of original remains). 2 × t1/2 = 60 min → t1/2 = 30 min.

Q8. Collision theory requirements.

Answer: (C) Proper orientation AND energy ≥ threshold

Both geometric (steric/orientation) and energetic (threshold energy) criteria must be satisfied for an effective collision.

Q9. Temperature coefficient when k doubles over 10 K.

Answer: (B) 2

Temperature coefficient μ = kT+10/kT. Given k doubles ⇒ μ = 2 (typical value 2–3).

Q10. Rate of NO₂ formation.

Answer: (C) 1.25 × 10⁻² mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹

Rate = −(1/2) d[N₂O₅]/dt = (1/4) d[NO₂]/dt. So d[NO₂]/dt = 2 × 6.25 × 10⁻³ = 1.25 × 10⁻² mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹.
