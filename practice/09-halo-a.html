Haloalkanes & Haloarenes — Answer Key

Q1. SN2 reactivity order.

Answer: (A) CH₃X > 1° > 2° > 3°

SN2 is a backside attack; steric crowding slows it. Methyl and 1° are fastest; 3° is so hindered that SN2 doesn't occur.

Q2. Finkelstein reaction.

Answer: (A) R-Cl + NaI in dry acetone → R-I + NaCl

NaCl is insoluble in acetone, NaI is soluble — equilibrium driven to the right by precipitation.

Q3. Swarts reaction.

Answer: (B) R-Br / R-Cl → R-F using AgF, Hg₂F₂ or SbF₃

Used to make alkyl fluorides. Direct F₂ would be too violent.

Q4. Chlorobenzene + Na (dry ether).

Answer: (B) Biphenyl (Fittig)

2 C₆H₅Cl + 2Na → C₆H₅-C₆H₅ + 2NaCl. Aryl-aryl coupling = Fittig reaction.

Q5. Diazonium + CuCl/HCl.

Answer: (A) Chlorobenzene (Sandmeyer)

ArN₂⁺ + CuCl/HCl → Ar-Cl + N₂. Standard Sandmeyer. CuBr/HBr gives Ar-Br; CuCN/KCN gives Ar-CN.

Q6. SN2 in polar aprotic solvents.

Answer: (A) Accelerated (nucleophile not solvated)

DMF, DMSO, acetone have no H-bond-donating O-H / N-H, so they don't solvate the anion. The nucleophile is "free" → stronger → faster SN2.

Q7. Most stable carbocation.

Answer: (D) (CH₃)₃C⁺ (tert-butyl cation)

3° cation is stabilised by hyperconjugation from 9 C–H bonds and +I from three methyl groups.

Q8. Chlorobenzene inert to nucleophiles.

Answer: (A) C–Cl bond has partial double-bond character (resonance)

Cl lone pair delocalises into the ring → C–Cl bond is shorter and stronger, and Cl leaves poorly unless activated by EW groups at o/p.

Q9. E2 major product of 2-bromo-2-methylbutane.

Answer: (B) 2-methyl-2-butene (Zaitsev)

Alc KOH favours E2. Zaitsev rule: the more-substituted alkene is the major (thermodynamic) product.

Q10. Solvent / fire-extinguisher.

Answer: (D) CCl₄

Carbon tetrachloride was used in "Pyrene" fire extinguishers and as industrial solvent. Its use is now restricted because of ozone layer damage and toxicity.
