Q1. Name of [Cr(NH₃)₄Cl₂]Cl.

Answer: (A) Tetraamminedichloridochromium(III) chloride

Alphabetical order: ammine (A) before chlorido (C). Cr OS: x + 4(0) + 2(−1) = +1 (outer Cl⁻), so x = +3.

Q2. CN and OS of Fe in K₃[Fe(C₂O₄)₃].

Answer: (B) CN = 6, OS = +3

Oxalate is bidentate → 3 × 2 = 6 donor atoms. Charge: 3(+1) + x + 3(−2) = 0 ⇒ x = +3.

Q3. Ambidentate ligand.

Answer: (C) NO₂⁻ / SCN⁻

Ambidentate has two different donor atoms, only one binds at a time: NO₂⁻ (N or O), SCN⁻ (S or N). en, C₂O₄²⁻ are chelating (bidentate); H₂O is unidentate.

Q4. Hybridisation of [NiCl₄]²⁻ and [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻.

Answer: (A) sp³ and dsp²

Cl⁻ is weak field → no pairing → 2 unpaired → sp³ tetrahedral, paramagnetic. CN⁻ strong field → pairs electrons → dsp² square planar, diamagnetic.

Q5. Optically active complex.

Answer: (B) [Co(en)₃]³⁺

Three bidentate en ligands create a non-superimposable chiral octahedral complex. [Co(NH₃)₆]³⁺ has a plane of symmetry; square planar Pt complex and tetrahedral [CoCl₄]²⁻ with identical ligands are achiral.

Q6. [Co(NH₃)₅Br]SO₄ vs [Co(NH₃)₅SO₄]Br.

Answer: (B) Ionisation isomers

Same formula, but ions inside vs outside the coordination sphere exchange → ionisation isomerism. On dissolving, the two give different ions (SO₄²⁻ vs Br⁻ free).

Q7. Octahedral d-orbital splitting.

Answer: (A) Lower t2g (3) and higher eg (2)

In octahedral field, eg (dz², dx²−y²) point at ligands (repulsion → higher E). t2g (dxy, dyz, dzx) lie between axes (lower E).

Q8. Unpaired e⁻ in [Fe(H₂O)₆]³⁺.

Answer: (A) 5

Fe³⁺ is d⁵. H₂O is weak field → high-spin → t2g³ eg² = 5 unpaired.

Q9. [Co(NH₃)₆]³⁺ nature.

Answer: (B) Low-spin, diamagnetic, d²sp³

Co³⁺ is d⁶. NH₃ strong field → pairing → t2g⁶ eg⁰, no unpaired electrons, inner orbital d²sp³ complex.

Q10. EDTA denticity.

Answer: (D) Hexadentate

EDTA⁴⁻ uses 2 N atoms and 4 COO⁻ O atoms → 6 donor sites → hexadentate, forms very stable octahedral chelates.
