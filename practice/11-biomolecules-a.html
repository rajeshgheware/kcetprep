Q1. HCN with glucose.

Answer: (B) Aldehyde group (-CHO)

HCN adds to C=O forming cyanohydrin. Only -CHO in glucose reacts. Acetic anhydride reaction would instead confirm 5 -OH.

Q2. Ketohexose.

Answer: (B) Fructose

Fructose is a 6-C sugar (hexose) with ketone group (C=O at C2). Glucose/galactose are aldohexoses; ribose is an aldopentose.

Q3. Sucrose non-reducing reason.

Answer: (B) Both anomeric C atoms are linked in the glycosidic bond

No free anomeric -OH → no open chain -CHO/-C=O can form → Fehling's/Tollens' negative.

Q4. Cellulose vs starch.

Answer: (B) Cellulose β-1,4; starch α-1,4

Humans lack β-glucosidase → can't digest cellulose (dietary fibre). Starch is digestible (α-1,4 cleaved by amylase).

Q5. Common protein 2° structure.

Answer: (A) α-helix

Right-handed α-helix stabilised by H-bonds between N-H of one amino acid and C=O four residues ahead. β-sheet also common but less frequent.

Q6. Zwitterion.

Answer: (A) -COOH donates H⁺ to -NH₂ in same molecule

Internal proton transfer: -COO⁻ and -NH₃⁺ coexist → dipolar ion explains high m.p., solubility in water, low solubility in ether.

Q7. Linkage between amino acids.

Answer: (B) Peptide (-CO-NH-)

Formed by condensation between -COOH of one amino acid and -NH₂ of next, with loss of H₂O.

Q8. Vitamin-disease match.

Answer: (C) Vitamin D — Rickets

Others: Vit A → night blindness; Vit C → scurvy; Vit K → bleeding/poor clotting; B₁ → beri-beri.

Q9. DNA base pairs.

Answer: (B) A-T and G-C

A pairs with T via 2 H-bonds; G pairs with C via 3 H-bonds. In RNA, U replaces T (A-U).

Q10. Sugar in RNA.

Answer: (B) D-ribose

RNA has D-ribose (2'-OH present). DNA has 2-deoxy-D-ribose (2'-H). This is the single-letter chemical difference driving their different stabilities.
