Aldehydes, Ketones & Carboxylic Acids — Answer Key

Q1. Tollens' gives Ag mirror with.

Answer: (B) Acetaldehyde

Tollens' oxidises aldehydes to carboxylate, Ag⁺ → Ag (mirror). Ketones, acids, nitriles give no reaction.

Q2. Cannizzaro reactant.

Answer: (C) Benzaldehyde

Cannizzaro requires an aldehyde with no α-hydrogen. PhCHO, HCHO, (CH₃)₃C-CHO qualify; acetaldehyde, propanal, 2-methylpropanal have α-H → undergo Aldol instead.

Q3. C=O → CH₂ under basic conditions.

Answer: (C) Wolff-Kishner reduction

NH₂NH₂/KOH in ethylene glycol reduces C=O to CH₂ under basic conditions. Clemmensen does the same but under acidic conditions (Zn-Hg/HCl).

Q4. CH₃CHO + dil NaOH → β-hydroxybutanal.

Answer: (B) Aldol condensation

α-carbon of one molecule attacks carbonyl carbon of another → β-hydroxy aldehyde (the "aldol"). Requires α-H.

Q5. HVZ product.

Answer: (A) α-halo carboxylic acid

R-CH₂-COOH + X₂/red P → R-CHX-COOH (α-halogenation). Phosphorus makes the acyl bromide intermediate; its enol reacts with X₂.

Q6. Rosenmund reduction.

Answer: (B) Acid chloride → aldehyde

R-COCl + H₂ with Pd-BaSO₄ (poisoned) stops at aldehyde; quinoline+S further poisons catalyst to avoid over-reduction.

Q7. Acid strength order.

Answer: (B) Cl₃CCOOH > Cl₂CHCOOH > ClCH₂COOH > CH₃COOH

More Cl (EW via −I) stabilises the carboxylate more → higher acidity. CH₃ is EDG so acetic is least acidic.

Q8. Friedel-Crafts acylation of benzene with CH₃COCl.

Answer: (C) Acetophenone (C₆H₅-CO-CH₃)

AlCl₃ makes acylium ion CH₃-C⁺=O, which attacks benzene → acetophenone.

Q9. HCHO + conc NaOH.

Answer: (B) Cannizzaro reaction

HCHO has no α-H → disproportionates: 2 HCHO + NaOH → HCOONa + CH₃OH (self-oxidation and self-reduction).

Q10. Acetone fails Fehling's.

Answer: (B) Ketones are not oxidised by mild oxidising agents

Fehling's is a mild oxidiser, can oxidise only aldehydic −CHO (→ −COOH). Ketones have no H on carbonyl carbon to lose in mild oxidation.
