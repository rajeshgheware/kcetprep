Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers — Answer Key

Score: ___ / 10

Q1. Immediate Lucas turbidity.

Answer: (C) tert-butyl alcohol (3°)

3° forms most stable carbocation → fast SN1 with HCl → immediate turbidity of 3° alkyl chloride.

Q2. Phenol > ethanol in acidity.

Answer: (A) Phenoxide resonance-stabilised

Negative charge on O delocalises into benzene ring (o, p positions) → stable phenoxide → phenol loses H⁺ more easily.

Q3. Reimer-Tiemann product.

Answer: (B) Salicylaldehyde

Phenol + CHCl₃ + NaOH (aq) → insertion of –CHO at ortho position → salicylaldehyde (2-hydroxybenzaldehyde) after hydrolysis.

Q4. Kolbe's reaction product.

Answer: (B) Salicylic acid

Sodium phenoxide + CO₂ at 400 K, 4–7 atm → sodium salicylate → acidified → salicylic acid (2-hydroxybenzoic acid).

Q5. Williamson for tert-butyl methyl ether.

Answer: (B) (CH₃)₃C–ONa + CH₃–I

Williamson is SN2. Use methyl halide (1°/unhindered) with bulky alkoxide. Option A would give elimination (E2 on 3° halide), not ether.

Q6. Oxidation of 2-butanol.

Answer: (C) Butanone

2-butanol is a secondary alcohol → oxidises to a ketone (butan-2-one).

Q7. Anisole + HI excess.

Answer: (B) Phenol (C₆H₅OH) and CH₃I

HI cleaves the C(aryl)–O bond less readily than C(alkyl)–O. Protonated anisole releases CH₃I (nucleophilic I⁻ attacks the CH₃). Aryl–O bond is not broken → phenol formed.

Q8. Phenol vs ethanol distinguishing test.

Answer: (B) Neutral FeCl₃ gives violet colour with phenol

Phenol forms a violet [Fe(OC₆H₅)₆]³⁻ complex. Alcohols do not give this colour. Both react with Na metal.

Q9. Acid hydration of propene.

Answer: (B) 2-propanol

Markovnikov: H⁺ adds to the C with more H's; water adds to the more-substituted C → secondary carbocation → 2-propanol (propan-2-ol).

Q10. Ease of dehydration.

Answer: (C) 3° > 2° > 1°

E1 goes via carbocation. 3° cation most stable → lowest Ea → fastest dehydration.
