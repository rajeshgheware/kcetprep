Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers — 10 MCQs

Target time: 13 min · Core chapter (14 Q across 2022–2025)
Key reactions:
  • Lucas test (anhyd ZnCl₂ + conc HCl): 3° → turbidity at once; 2° → 5 min; 1° → none at RT
  • Phenol: Kolbe's (NaOH, CO₂, 400 K, 4–7 atm) → salicylic acid; Reimer-Tiemann (NaOH + CHCl₃) → salicylaldehyde
  • Dehydration ease: 3° > 2° > 1° (E1, carbocation stability); Oxidation of alcohols: 1° → aldehyde → acid, 2° → ketone, 3° no common oxidation
  • Williamson: R–ONa + R'X → R–O–R' (best with 1° alkyl halide, SN2); Ether cleavage with HI: usually CH₃–O–R + HI → CH₃I + ROH
