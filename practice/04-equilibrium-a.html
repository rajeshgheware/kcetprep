Q1. Kp/Kc for N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃.

Answer: (B) Kp = Kc(RT)⁻²

Δn = 2 − (1+3) = −2 ⇒ Kp = Kc(RT)⁻².

Q2. Shift for 2NO₂ ⇌ N₂O₄.

Answer: (C) Increase in pressure

Δngas = 1 − 2 = −1. Higher pressure favours fewer moles → shifts forward (to N₂O₄). Inert gas at constant V doesn't change partial pressures.

Q3. pH of 0.001 M HCl.

Answer: (C) 3

[H⁺] = 10⁻³ M → pH = −log(10⁻³) = 3.

Q4. Buffer pH for equal [acid] = [salt].

Answer: (B) 4.74

pH = pKa + log(1) = pKa = −log(1.8 × 10⁻⁵) = 4.74.

Q5. Ksp of AgCl.

Answer: (B) 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁰

AgCl ⇌ Ag⁺ + Cl⁻; Ksp = s² = (10⁻⁵)² = 10⁻¹⁰.

Q6. NH₄Cl solution nature.

Answer: (B) Acidic, NH₄⁺ hydrolyses

Salt of strong acid + weak base. NH₄⁺ + H₂O ⇌ NH₃ + H₃O⁺. Cl⁻ (conjugate of strong HCl) does not hydrolyse.

Q7. Ca(OH)₂ solubility decreases in NaOH.

Answer: (A) Common ion effect

Common OH⁻ shifts Ca(OH)₂ ⇌ Ca²⁺ + 2OH⁻ to the left, decreasing solubility. Ksp is unchanged.

Q8. Valid conjugate acid/base pair.

Answer: (C) NH₄⁺ / NH₃

NH₄⁺ loses H⁺ to give NH₃. (A), (B), (D) are not conjugate pairs (they differ by more than one H⁺).

Q9. pH of 0.1 M weak acid, Ka = 10⁻⁵.

Answer: (B) 3

[H⁺] = √(Ka·C) = √(10⁻⁵ × 0.1) = √10⁻⁶ = 10⁻³. pH = 3.

Q10. What does NOT change Kc.

Answer: (B) Change in concentration of reactants

Kc depends only on temperature (and the specific reaction). Concentration changes shift the equilibrium position but not Kc.
