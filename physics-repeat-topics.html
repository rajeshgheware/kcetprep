AtomsBohr orbit radius scales as n²

rn = n² × 0.529 Å = n² × r1 ·   En = −13.6/n² eV

"If 1st Bohr radius is r, then 2nd orbit radius is?" → 4r. "Ratio of radii of n=2 to n=1?" → 4:1.

2 · 6/6

Oscillations (SHM)KE = PE happens at x = A/√2

Etot = ½mω²A² ·   KE = ½mω²(A²−x²) ·   PE = ½mω²x²
KE = PE ⇒ x = A/√2 ·   KE = PE = E/2

Also: at x = 0 → KE max; at x = ±A → PE max. T_spring = 2π√(m/k); T_pendulum = 2π√(ℓ/g).

Alternating CurrentLCR impedance + V across LC

Z = √(R² + (XL − XC)²) ·   i = V/Z
VLC = i·|XL − XC| ·   Vrms = V₀/√2
Resonance: ω₀ = 1/√(LC), Z = R (min)

"V across L+C only in series LCR" → i × |X_L−X_C|, NOT V₀. cos φ = R/Z (power factor).

Moving Charges & MagnetismLong wire B and parallel-wire force

B = μ₀I / (2πr) · graph B vs r → rectangular hyperbola
F/ℓ between parallel wires = μ₀I₁I₂ / (2πr)
same direction → attract; opposite → repel

Solenoid: B = μ₀nI (inside, uniform). Toroid: B = μ₀NI/(2πr).

EMISelf-inductance and induced EMF

ε = −L (dI/dt) ·   φ = LI ·   U = ½LI²
Lsolenoid = μ₀n²Aℓ
Lenz's law: induced current opposes change in φ

Bar magnet pushed INTO coil → galvanometer deflects one way; pulled OUT → deflects opposite way.

SemiconductorsDiode, Zener, LED

Si diode Vknee ≈ 0.7 V · Ge ≈ 0.3 V
Ideal diode: FB → short (0 Ω); RB → open (∞ Ω)
Zener: regulates in reverse breakdown
LED: Eg = hc/λ (band-gap sets colour)

n-type: donor level just below conduction band. p-type: acceptor level just above valence band.

7 · 5/6

ElectrostaticsConducting sphere — E and V

Inside (r < R): E = 0, V = kQ/R (constant)
Outside (r ≥ R): E = kQ/r², V = kQ/r
Graph: E jumps from 0 at R, falls as 1/r²

Non-conducting uniform sphere: inside E ∝ r (rises), outside same as conducting.

8 · 5/6

Dual Naturede Broglie wavelength

λ = h/p = h/√(2mEk) = h/√(2mqV)
Same λ → same momentum p (NOT same v, KE, or E)

"Electron & photon of same λ have same...?" → momentum. Photon E = hc/λ; electron E = p²/2m.

Dual NaturePhotoelectric effect

KEmax = hν − φ = h(ν − ν₀)
eVstop = KEmax
Iphoto ∝ intensity (for ν > ν₀); independent of ν
Vstop independent of intensity

Graph V_stop vs ν → straight line, slope = h/e, x-intercept = ν₀.

10 · 4/6

ElectrostaticsGauss law — flux tricks

Φ = qenc / ε₀ (closed surface only)
Point charge at cube centre → Φ = q/ε₀; per face = q/(6ε₀)
Point charge at cube corner → Φ = q/(8ε₀)
Shielded/external charges → Φ = 0

Electrostatic shielding: flux through inner region due to outer charge = 0.

11 · 4/6

Ray OpticsLens combinations & cut lens

In contact: 1/f = 1/f₁ + 1/f₂; P = P₁ + P₂ (dioptre)
Lens cut ⟂ principal axis (halves) → each half has P/2
Lens cut ‖ axis → each half still has power P
P = 1/f (metres); lens-maker's 1/f = (μ−1)(1/R₁ − 1/R₂)

Immersion in water: f increases (lens becomes weaker); if μlens = μmedium, lens vanishes.

12 · 4/6

Ray OpticsPrism — μ from min deviation

r₁ + r₂ = A (always) · δ = (i₁ + i₂) − A
At min deviation: i₁ = i₂ and r₁ = r₂ = A/2
μ = sin((A + δm)/2) / sin(A/2)

"Angle of incidence is 2× angle of refraction, find i in terms of n" → use sin i = n sin r, 2 cos(r) = n, so i = 2 cos⁻¹(n/2).

13 · 4/6

Magnetism & MatterCurie's law + dia/para/ferro

Paramagnetic: χ = C/T (positive, small, ↓ with T)
Diamagnetic: χ negative, small, T-independent
Ferromagnetic: χ very large, positive, ↓ above TC

"Susceptibility of diamagnetic substance is..." → small and negative.

14 · 3/6

Wave OpticsMalus' law (polaroids)

Unpolarised → polaroid: I = I₀/2
Polarised → polaroid at angle θ: I = I₀ cos²θ
Two polaroids at 90° → I = 0
Three polaroids (0°, θ, 90°): I = (I₀/2) cos²θ sin²θ

For 3-polaroid max transmission, middle at 45° → Imax = I₀/8.

15 · 3/6

NucleiHalf-life, decay & β-emission

N = N₀ e−λt ·   t½ = ln 2 / λ = 0.693/λ
Activity A = λN
Fraction remaining after n half-lives = (½)n
β⁻ decay: A unchanged, Z → Z + 1 (neutron → proton)
α decay: A → A − 4, Z → Z − 2

Nuclear force: attractive > 0.8 fm, repulsive < 0.8 fm, short-range (~few fm).
