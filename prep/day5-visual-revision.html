ORGANIC FINISHERS — Hydrocarbons + GOC~20 Q / 8 yrs combined

Alkenes & alkynes — the must-know reactions

Markovnikov: H goes to the C with more H. Anti-Markovnikov: only with HBr + peroxide (free-radical mechanism), not HCl or HI.
Baeyer's test: cold dil. alkaline KMnO₄ decolourises (purple → brown MnO₂ ppt) with alkenes/alkynes. Distinguishes unsaturation from alkanes.
Alkyne acidity: terminal HC≡C–H has acidic H (sp ~50% s-character). Na/NaNH₂ forms acetylide → SN2 with RX → longer alkyne.
Ozonolysis: reductive (Zn/H₂O) gives ald/ket. Oxidative (H₂O₂) gives acid/ketone. Used to locate double bond.
Alkyne + H₂O (HgSO₄/H₂SO₄): HC≡CH → CH₃CHO (Markovnikov, enol tautomerises). Internal alkyne → ketone.

o,p-directors (activators): –OH, –OR, –NH₂, –NHR, –NR₂, –R, halogens*. *Halogens activate o,p but are deactivators overall (–I > +M).
m-directors (deactivators): –NO₂, –CN, –SO₃H, –COOH, –COR, –CHO, –NR₃⁺.
Friedel-Crafts fails on strongly deactivated rings (nitrobenzene, aniline-as-ammonium) and in presence of –NH₂ (AlCl₃ complexes with N lone pair).
Memorise the trick: lone pair donors o,p; multiple bonds to O/N m. The single exception is halogens (o,p but deactivating).

IUPAC naming — the 5-step algorithm

1. Longest continuous C chain containing the highest-priority group
2. Number so principal group gets lowest locant
3. Name substituents alphabetically (ignore di/tri prefixes)
4. Indicate double/triple bonds with lowest locants
5. Principal group as suffix (-oic acid > -oate > -amide > -nitrile > -al > -one > -ol > -amine)
Priority for suffix (high → low): COOH > COOR > CONH₂ > CN > CHO > CO > OH > NH₂. All others (halogen, –NO₂, –OR, –R) are prefixes only.
Common prefixes: F=fluoro, Cl=chloro, Br=bromo, I=iodo, NO₂=nitro, OR=alkoxy, R=alkyl.
Tie-break for locants: principal group first, then unsaturation, then substituents as a set, then alphabetical set.

Lone pair > bond pair in repulsion → compresses bond angles. H₂O (104.5°) < NH₃ (107°) < CH₄ (109.5°).
Trigonal bipyramidal: LPs always go in equatorial positions (minimum repulsion) — remember SF₄ see-saw, not trigonal pyramidal.

MOT for diatomics & bond order

Bond order = (N_b − N_a) / 2
O₂: BO = 2, paramagnetic (2 unpaired e⁻ in π* — explained only by MOT, not VBT).
N₂: BO = 3, diamagnetic, shortest N–N bond (110 pm).
Species comparison: N₂ (3) > N₂⁺ (2.5) = N₂⁻ (2.5) > N₂²⁻ (2). Higher BO → shorter, stronger bond.
Noble-gas-like stability: He₂ has BO = 0 (1 bonding, 1 antibonding) — no He₂ molecule. Be₂ same, BO = 0.

Dipole moment & H-bonding

μ = q × d   (D, debye)   μ_net = vector sum of bond dipoles
Non-polar despite polar bonds: CO₂ (linear, cancels), BF₃ (trigonal planar), CCl₄ (tetrahedral), SF₆ (octahedral).
H-bonding needs: H bonded to F, O, or N + lone pair acceptor. Ranking F–H...F > O–H...O > N–H...N.
Effects of H-bonding: ↑ boiling point (HF, H₂O, NH₃ are anomalously high), ↑ solubility in water, ↑ viscosity (glycerol), ↓ volatility. Ice floats because H-bonds create open tetrahedral lattice.
Intramolecular H-bond: o-nitrophenol (lower BP than p-isomer — cannot form intermolecular H-bond).
Fajan's rules: covalent character ↑ with (a) small cation, (b) large anion, (c) high cation charge, (d) d¹⁰ cation (Cu⁺, Ag⁺). e.g., AgCl more covalent than NaCl.

Redox — oxidation numbers & balancing

Rules: F = −1 always; O = −2 (except peroxides −1, superoxides −½, OF₂ +2); H = +1 (except hydrides −1)
Tricky OS: Cr in K₂Cr₂O₇ = +6; Mn in KMnO₄ = +7; S in Na₂S₂O₃ = +2 (avg); N in NH₄NO₃ = −3 & +5.
Half-reaction balancing (acidic): balance atoms other than O/H → balance O with H₂O → balance H with H⁺ → balance charge with e⁻ → multiply to cancel e⁻.
Basic medium: balance as acidic, then add OH⁻ equal to H⁺ on both sides, convert H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O, cancel.
Types: combination, decomposition, displacement (single), disproportionation (same species oxidised + reduced, e.g., 2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂; Cl₂ in NaOH), comproportionation (two OSs merge).
Equivalent weight = M/n. For KMnO₄ in acid: n = 5 → eq wt = 158/5 = 31.6. In neutral: n = 3 → 52.7. In base: n = 1 → 158.
PHYSICAL FOUNDATIONS — Thermo · Atomic · Periodicity · Mole~14 Q / 8 yrs combined

Thermodynamics — ΔH, ΔS, ΔG and spontaneity

ΔG = ΔH − TΔS   (at constant T, P)
First law: ΔU = q + w. At constant V: q_v = ΔU. At constant P: q_p = ΔH. ΔH = ΔU + Δn_gas · RT.
Hess's law: ΔH is state function — sum across steps.
Key signs: exothermic → ΔH −ve; entropy of gas > liquid > solid; dissolution usually ΔS +ve.
ΔG° = −RT ln K at equilibrium. At equilibrium ΔG = 0 (not ΔG°). Spontaneity is about ΔG, not ΔG°.

Aufbau: fill lowest (n + l) first; tie → lower n. Order: 1s → 2s → 2p → 3s → 3p → 4s → 3d → 4p → 5s → 4d → 5p → 6s → 4f → 5d → 6p ...
Hund's rule: fill each orbital of a subshell singly before pairing.
Pauli: no two electrons in an atom share all 4 QNs.
Extra-stable configs: d⁵ and d¹⁰ (half / full). Cr = [Ar] 3d⁵ 4s¹ (not 3d⁴ 4s²). Cu = [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s¹.

Periodicity — trends & exceptions

Down a group: radius ↑, IE ↓, EA less −ve, EN ↓, metallic ↑. Extra shells override nuclear pull.
IE anomalies: B < Be (2p vs 2s), O < N (paired p electron repulsion), Al < Mg (same reasons one row down). Remember these dips.
EA anomaly: F < Cl (F's small 2p is electron-crowded). Cl has the most negative EA.
Diagonal relationships: Li–Mg, Be–Al, B–Si. Similar size & polarizing power despite different groups.
Pauling EN scale: F = 4.0 (max), Cs = 0.7 (min). Key numbers: O 3.5, N 3.0, Cl 3.0, C 2.5, H 2.1.

Mole concept — the conversion hub

n = m / M = N / N_A = V_gas / 22.4 (STP, L) = V × C (solution, L × M)
Molarity M: mol solute / L solution. Molality m: mol solute / kg solvent. Mole fraction x: mol component / total mol.
Empirical formula: divide % by atomic mass → divide by smallest → simplest ratio. Molecular formula: (empirical formula)ₙ where n = M_mol / M_empirical.
Limiting reagent: divide moles by stoichiometric coefficient → smallest quotient is limiting.
% yield: (actual / theoretical) × 100. Atom economy: (M of desired product / total M of reactants) × 100.
STP values: T = 273.15 K, P = 1 bar (not 1 atm in modern NCERT). 1 mol gas = 22.7 L at new STP (22.4 L at old). Problem statements usually specify.
