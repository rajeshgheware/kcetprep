SOLUTIONS28 Q / 8 yrs · 3.5 per paper

Raoult's law — ideal binary solution

Ptotal = xAP°A + xBP°B
Non-volatile solute: ΔP/P° = xsolute (relative lowering = mole fraction of solute)

Positive vs negative deviation

+ve A–B weaker than A–A/B–B → min BP azeotrope
−ve A–B stronger (H-bond) → max BP azeotrope (HCl+H₂O, CHCl₃+acetone)

Osmotic pressure — U-tube

π = i C R T   (C in mol/L, T in K)
Isotonic = same π (0.9% NaCl is iso with blood). Hypertonic shrinks cells; hypotonic bursts them.

ΔTb / ΔTf — colligative

ΔTb = i·Kb·m  ·  ΔTf = i·Kf·m
Solute adds ↓vp → BP goes up, FP goes down. Camphor has huge Kf (40) → Rast method.

Henry's law & temperature

pgas = KH · xgas
KH ↑ with T → gas solubility ↓ with T. Warm soda goes flat; aquatic life suffers in warm water.

ELECTROCHEMISTRY27 Q / 8 yrs · 3.4 per paper

Galvanic (Daniell) cell

Anode (−): Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻   Cathode (+): Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu
E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode = +1.10 V
Galvanic = spontaneous (ΔG < 0, E° > 0). Electrons flow anode → cathode in external wire; cations move to cathode through salt bridge.

Nernst equation & ΔG ↔ E° ↔ K

E = E° − (0.059/n) log Q   (at 298 K)
E° > 0 ⇔ ΔG° < 0 ⇔ K > 1 ⇔ spontaneous

Conductance relations ↗

Strong electrolytes: Λm vs √C is linear → extrapolate to get Λ°m.
Weak electrolytes: can't extrapolate → use Kohlrausch: Λ°m(CH₃COOH) = Λ°m(CH₃COONa) + Λ°m(HCl) − Λ°m(NaCl)
Degree of ionisation α = Λm / Λ°m

Faraday's laws of electrolysis

1 Faraday (F) = 96 500 C deposits 1 mol equivalent (M/n grams).
Ag⁺ + e⁻ → Ag needs 1 F per mol Ag. Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu needs 2 F per mol Cu.
Mass ratio for same charge: mA/mB = (EA / EB) where E = M/n is equivalent mass.

Electrochemical series (selected)

Higher (more +ve) E° = stronger oxidising agent (gets reduced easily). Li is the strongest reducing agent; F₂ the strongest oxidising agent.

Rate vs time — order shapes

Straight line of [A] vs t → zero order · ln[A] vs t → first order · 1/[A] vs t → second order

Nuclear decay ≈ 1st order → half-life constant; useful for C-14 dating.

Arrhenius equation

k = A e−Ea/RT  ·  ln(k₂/k₁) = (Ea/R)(1/T₁ − 1/T₂)
T ↑ → k ↑↑. Temp coefficient μ = kT+10/kT ≈ 2–3 (rate doubles/triples per 10 K).

Activation energy (with vs without catalyst)

Catalyst lowers Ea, does NOT change ΔH or K. Speeds both forward and reverse equally.

Collision theory requirements

Rate = ZAB · p · e−Ea/RT   (p = steric factor)
