COORDINATION COMPOUNDS25 Q / 8 yrs · 3.1 per paper

IUPAC nomenclature — the 5-step recipe

1. Cation before anion (same as simple salts).
2. Inside bracket: ligands alphabetical (ignore di/tri prefixes when ordering).
3. Anionic ligands end in -o: Cl = chlorido, OH = hydroxido, CN = cyanido, SO₄ = sulfato.
4. Neutral ligands: NH₃ = ammine, H₂O = aqua, CO = carbonyl, NO = nitrosyl.
5. If complex is anion, metal ends in -ate: Fe → ferrate, Cu → cuprate, Ag → argentate, Au → aurate, Pb → plumbate.
Use bis, tris, tetrakis when ligand name already contains di/tri/tetra (e.g., bis(ethylenediamine)).

Isomerism — structural + stereo

Structural: ionisation [Co(NH₃)₅Br]SO₄ vs [Co(NH₃)₅SO₄]Br · hydrate [Cr(H₂O)₆]Cl₃ vs [Cr(H₂O)₅Cl]Cl₂·H₂O · linkage (NO₂/ONO, SCN/NCS) · coordination [Co(NH₃)₆][Cr(CN)₆].
Stereo: geometrical (cis/trans) · optical (d/l enantiomers, e.g., [Co(en)₃]³⁺).
Octahedral MA₄B₂ has cis/trans · tetrahedral has no cis/trans · [M(en)₃] chiral.

CFT — octahedral vs tetrahedral splitting

Octahedral: d-orbitals split into lower t₂g (3) + upper e_g (2). Energy: t₂g = −0.4Δ_o each, e_g = +0.6Δ_o each.
Tetrahedral: inverted — lower e (2) + upper t₂ (3). Δ_t ≈ (4/9)Δ_o.
CFSE = (−0.4 × nt₂g + 0.6 × ne_g) Δ_o. Tetrahedral complexes are always high-spin (Δ_t too small to pair).

Spectrochemical series & spin state

Weak field (H₂O, halides, F⁻): Δ_o < P → electrons don't pair → high-spin, more unpaired, more paramagnetic.
Strong field (CN⁻, CO, en): Δ_o > P → electrons pair up in t₂g → low-spin, fewer unpaired, often diamagnetic.
Same metal+OS, change ligand → colour, μ, geometry can all flip. [Fe(H₂O)₆]³⁺ high-spin (5 unpaired) vs [Fe(CN)₆]³⁻ low-spin (1 unpaired).

Colour origin: d-d transitions. White light minus absorbed λ = observed colour.
Ligand strength ↑ → Δ_o ↑ → absorbs shorter λ → colour shifts. [Ti(H₂O)₆]³⁺ purple; [Cu(H₂O)₄]²⁺ pale blue → [Cu(NH₃)₄]²⁺ deep blue.

d & f-BLOCK ELEMENTS27 Q / 8 yrs · 3.4 per paper

General trends across 3d series

Density: increases across (max around Cu/Ni zone). Osmium is densest element.
MP/BP: high in middle (Cr, Mo, W highest), low at Zn (filled 3d¹⁰ → no d-electron bonding).
IE: gradually increases; ns and (n−1)d electrons comparable.
Why trends flatten? Added d-electrons poorly shield → effective nuclear charge rises slowly → size changes little after V.

Lanthanoid & Actinoid contraction

Cause: 4f electrons shield very poorly → effective nuclear charge ↑ as Z ↑.
Consequences: (1) Zr and Hf have nearly identical radii (impossible to separate). (2) 4d and 5d transition metals have similar properties. (3) Basicity of Ln hydroxides decreases La → Lu.
Actinoid contraction is larger (5f electrons shield even worse than 4f). +3 is stable for all Ln; actinoids show +3 to +7.

KMnO₄ — oxidation in 3 media

Acidic: MnO₄⁻ + 8H⁺ + 5e⁻ → Mn²⁺ + 4H₂O   (E° = +1.51 V)
Prep: MnO₂ fused with KOH + air → K₂MnO₄ (green) → electrolytic oxidation → KMnO₄ (purple).
Acidic is most strongly oxidising (n=5). Use dil. H₂SO₄ (not HCl — HCl gets oxidised).

K₂Cr₂O₇ — structure & chromate equilibrium

2 CrO₄²⁻ + 2H⁺ ⇌ Cr₂O₇²⁻ + H₂O  (yellow ⇌ orange)
Cr₂O₇²⁻ + 14H⁺ + 6e⁻ → 2Cr³⁺ + 7H₂O  (E° = +1.33 V, n = 6)
Prep: FeCr₂O₄ + Na₂CO₃/air → Na₂CrO₄ → Na₂Cr₂O₇ (acid) → K₂Cr₂O₇ (KCl metathesis).
Chromate (yellow) in base ⇌ dichromate (orange) in acid. Never add HCl to KMnO₄/K₂Cr₂O₇ for titration — oxidises Cl⁻.
