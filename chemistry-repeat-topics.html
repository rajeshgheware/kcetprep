1 · 6/6

Coordination CompoundsIUPAC naming of complexes

Ligands: alphabetical, anionic then neutral order dropped (2005 IUPAC). Cation first, anion second.
-ate suffix if whole complex is anion (e.g. cobaltate, ferrate).
Oxidation state of metal in Roman numerals after metal name.

[Co(NH₃)₅(CO₃)]Cl = Pentaamminecarbonatocobalt(III) chloride. K₃[Fe(CN)₆] = Potassium hexacyanoferrate(III). Watch out: "chloro" before "ammine" — C before N.

Coordination + d-BlockHybridisation, magnetism, colour

μ = √[n(n + 2)] BM · n = unpaired electrons
Strong-field ligands (CN⁻, CO, NO₂⁻, en, NH₃) → pair up → low-spin → inner-orbital (d²sp³)
Weak-field (F⁻, Cl⁻, H₂O, OH⁻, ox) → high-spin → outer (sp³d²)
d⁰ or d¹⁰ ions (Sc³⁺, Ti⁴⁺, Zn²⁺, Cu⁺) are colourless.

[Ni(CN)₄]²⁻ = dsp² (square planar, diamagnetic). [NiCl₄]²⁻ = sp³ (tetrahedral, paramagnetic). μ = √24 BM ⇒ 4 unpaired ⇒ Fe²⁺ or Co³⁺.

ElectrochemistryKohlrausch law + molar conductivity

Λ°m(AB) = λ°+ + λ°− (sum of ionic limits)
For weak electrolyte: α = Λm / Λ°m; Ka = cα²/(1−α)
Λ°(CH₃COOH) = λ°(Na⁺) + λ°(CH₃COO⁻) + λ°(HCl) − λ°(NaCl)

Strong electrolyte: Λm = Λ°m − A√c (Debye-Hückel-Onsager). Unit: S cm² mol⁻¹.

4 · 5/6

ElectrochemistryNernst equation & K from E°

At 298 K: Ecell = E°cell − (0.0591/n) log Q
Q = [products]/[reactants] for cell reaction
At equilibrium: E = 0 ⇒ log K = (n · E°) / 0.0591
ΔG° = −nFE° = −2.303RT log K

Concentration cell: E° = 0, E = (0.0591/n) log(c₂/c₁). Faraday: Q = n × 96 500 C to deposit 1 mol (Ag=1F, Mg²⁺=2F, Al³⁺=3F).

Chemical KineticsFirst-order integrated form

k = (2.303/t) log(a / (a − x)) ·   t½ = 0.693 / k
t99% = 4.606/k = 2 × t90% = 10 × t½ (approx)
Units of k: order 0 → M s⁻¹; order 1 → s⁻¹; order 2 → M⁻¹s⁻¹

"Time to complete 99.9% reaction" = (2.303/k) log 1000 = 6.909/k. For first order, t½ is independent of [A]₀.

6 · 5/6

Chemical KineticsArrhenius equation

k = A · e−Ea/RT ·   ln k = ln A − Ea/(RT)
log(k₂/k₁) = (Ea / 2.303R) · (1/T₁ − 1/T₂)
A = frequency / pre-exponential factor (collisions)
Catalyst lowers Ea only; does NOT change ΔH, ΔG, or Keq.

Temp rise of 10 K usually doubles rate (Ea ≈ 50 kJ/mol). Plot: slope of ln k vs 1/T = −Ea/R.

7 · 5/6

SolutionsColligative: ΔTb, ΔTf, π

ΔTb = i · Kb · m ·   ΔTf = i · Kf · m
π = i · C · R · T (osmotic) · psolvent = xsolv · p° (Raoult)
van't Hoff factor: glucose = 1; NaCl = 2; K₂SO₄ = 3; Al₂(SO₄)₃ = 5; CaCl₂ = 3
Association (dimer) → i < 1; dissociation → i > 1

"Equimolal NaCl vs glucose — which has higher ΔTb?" → NaCl (i = 2). Isotonic: equal π at same T.

8 · 5/6

Amines / HaloarenesDiazonium reactions (Sandmeyer etc.)

ArNH₂ → ArN₂⁺Cl⁻ via NaNO₂/HCl at 0–5 °C
Sandmeyer: CuCl/CuBr/CuCN → ArCl / ArBr / ArCN
Gatterman: Cu/HCl or Cu/HBr → ArCl / ArBr (cheaper)
Balz-Schiemann: ArN₂⁺BF₄⁻ → ArF (heat)
ArN₂⁺ + H₂O/H⁺, warm → ArOH  ·  + H₃PO₂ → ArH (deamination)

ArN₂⁺ + phenol → azo dye (coupling, p-HO-C₆H₄-N=N-Ar, orange). + β-naphthol → red dye.

9 · 5/6

BiomoleculesCarbohydrates & tests

Reducing sugars: glucose, fructose, maltose, lactose → +ve Tollens / Fehling / Benedict
Non-reducing: sucrose (α-1,β-2 glycosidic), starch, cellulose → −ve
Sucrose hydrolysis → glucose + fructose; rotation changes +66.5° → −20° (invert sugar)
Glucose: open-chain & α / β pyranose (1-OH down/up); anomers differ only at C-1.

Ring size: 6-member = pyranose; 5-member = furanose. Fructose reduces Tollens' via tautomerism (α-hydroxy ketone in basic medium).

10 · 5/6

d & f-BlockLanthanoid contraction & consequences

Atomic/ionic radii ↓ across 4f series (poor shielding of f-e)
Consequences:
• 5d radii ≈ 4d (Zr ≈ Hf, Nb ≈ Ta, Mo ≈ W)
• Basic strength of Ln(OH)₃ ↓ from La → Lu
• Hardness, density, m.p. ↑ across Ln series
Common OS = +3 (4f⁰, 4f⁷, 4f¹⁴ extra-stable)

Exception OS: Ce⁴⁺ (4f⁰), Eu²⁺ (4f⁷), Tb⁴⁺ (4f⁷), Yb²⁺ (4f¹⁴). Pm is radioactive (only Ln with no stable isotope).

11 · 4/6

AminesHinsberg + basicity order

Hinsberg: C₆H₅SO₂Cl (benzenesulfonyl chloride)
1° amine → soluble in KOH (N-H acidic after sulfonamide)
2° amine → insoluble in KOH (no N-H in sulfonamide)
3° amine → no reaction
Basicity in gas phase: 3° > 2° > 1° > NH₃ (pure +I)
Basicity in water: 2° > 1° ≈ 3° > NH₃ (solvation + steric)

Aromatic < aliphatic (lone pair in ring). p-anisidine > aniline > p-nitroaniline. Carbylamine test: 1° only, RNH₂ + CHCl₃ + KOH → RNC (foul).

12 · 4/6

Aldehydes / KetonesAldol vs Cannizzaro

Aldol: needs α-H. 2 RCHO + dil NaOH → β-hydroxy aldehyde → heat → α,β-unsat carbonyl
Cannizzaro: no α-H. 2 PhCHO + conc. NaOH → PhCH₂OH + PhCOO⁻ (disproportionation)
No α-H → HCHO, PhCHO, Cl₃C-CHO, Me₃C-CHO (pivaldehyde)
Crossed aldol / crossed Cannizzaro with HCHO as H-donor.

"Which does NOT undergo aldol?" → the one with no α-H. HCHO with conc. NaOH → CH₃OH + HCOO⁻ (Cannizzaro).

13 · 4/6

Aldehydes / KetonesEtard, Stephen, Rosenmund — RCHO prep

Etard: toluene + CrO₂Cl₂ / CS₂ → chromium complex → H₃O⁺ → PhCHO
Stephen: R-CN + SnCl₂ / HCl → aldimine → H₃O⁺ → RCHO
Rosenmund: R-COCl + H₂ / Pd-BaSO₄ (poisoned) → RCHO (stops at aldehyde)
Gatterman-Koch: benzene + CO + HCl / AlCl₃, CuCl → PhCHO

Oxidation of 1° alcohol with PCC (mild) → aldehyde; with KMnO₄ / K₂Cr₂O₇ → carboxylic acid. DIBAL-H on ester/nitrile → aldehyde.

14 · 4/6

Alcohols / EthersEther cleavage with HI

R-O-R' + HI (excess, heat) → R-I + R'-OH (further → R'-I + H₂O)
SN2 mechanism → I⁻ attacks less hindered C (smaller alkyl gets iodide)
Phenyl ether (Ph-O-R) → Ph-OH + R-I (phenyl-O bond stronger; Csp²-O not broken)

Anisole (C₆H₅-O-CH₃) + HI → phenol + CH₃I. tert-butyl methyl ether + HI → tert-butyl iodide + CH₃OH (SN1 on 3° C).

15 · 4/6

HaloalkanesWurtz + Fittig + SN1/SN2 quick

Wurtz: 2 R-X + 2 Na / dry ether → R-R + 2 NaX
Wurtz-Fittig: Ar-X + R-X + 2 Na / ether → Ar-R (alkylbenzene)
Fittig: 2 Ar-X + 2 Na → Ar-Ar (biphenyl)
SN1: 3° > 2° > 1°; polar protic; racemisation
SN2: 1° > 2° > 3°; polar aprotic (DMSO, DMF); inversion

Saytzeff (E1/E2): major product = more substituted alkene. Hofmann (bulky base, NaOEt with t-BuOK): less substituted alkene.
