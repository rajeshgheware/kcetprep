Post-rationalisation syllabus — what to skip. The 2023 NCERT rationalisation removed whole chunks from Physics. Communication Systems is gone entirely. Semiconductors is pared down — no logic gates, no transistor-as-amplifier, no transistor characteristics. In Class 11, Physical World is dropped; reduced coverage in Vectors (scalar/vector product derivations), Heat engines & Carnot cycle, and parts of Radioactive decay. The KCET-2025 paper honoured this — zero questions on any removed area. Do NOT revise removed content.

Electromagnetism (23 Q)

Electrostatics + Current + Magnetism + EMI + AC. The single biggest cluster — ~38% of the paper.

Mechanics Class 11 (11 Q)

Kinematics + Laws + WEP + Rotation + Gravitation. Formula-heavy, one-step numerics — easy scoring.

Optics + Modern (15 Q)

Ray 5 + Wave 2 + Dual 2 + Atoms 1 + Nuclei 2 + Semi 3. Definition & formula recall — high yield.

Supporting (9 Q)

Properties of Matter (Solids/Fluids), Thermal, Thermo, KTG, SHM, Waves, Units. One Q each — don't over-prep.

What the data says

Electromagnetism is the paper. 23 of 60 Qs in 2025 came from Electrostatics → AC. If you own this cluster cold, you lock ~18–20 marks before touching anything else. Priority #1.
Optics + Modern = 15 more Qs. These are definition & one-step formula questions (Bohr radius, photocell graph, EM wavelength ranges, lens-maker, prism, n-type donor level). Formula sheet + 30 flash recalls clinches 10+ marks.
Class 11 Mechanics = 11 Qs, all two-line numerics. Friction, projectile, WE theorem, CoM, Kepler, Bernoulli, SHM energy. JEE-level is overkill — KCET stays strictly NCERT.
JEE Phys was 77.50 percentile — conceptually OK, unit-discipline and graph-reading are the leverage. ~8 Qs in 2025 were graph-based (ρ–T, R–D, B–r, photocell I–ν, KE/PE SHM, E–r outside sphere). Practice reading graphs fast.
Two experiment-style Qs appeared in 2025 (half-deflection galvanometer Q55, viscosity by sinking ball Q56). Skim NCERT Lab-Manual "Activities" — short formulas, no full procedure needed.

Tactics (how to study)

  1. One-page formula sheet per cluster. EM cluster = 1 page (Gauss forms, Kirchhoff, B for wire/loop/solenoid, Faraday, LCR). Optics = 1 page. Modern = 1 page. Mechanics = 1 page. Re-read daily.
  2. Graph recognition. Memorise the shape of: E vs r (conducting / non-cond. sphere), V vs r, B vs r (long wire), R vs T (metal / semi), I vs ν (photocell), φ vs θ (dipole), KE/PE vs x in SHM.
  3. Units first. In every numeric, convert to SI before plugging in. Dimensional check > memorising formulas.
  4. NCERT statements. For Magnetism-&-Matter, EM-Waves, Semiconductors, Nuclei — KCET lifts statements verbatim. Read those 4 chapters line-by-line once.
  5. Mock cadence: 80 min / 60 Q = 80 sec each. 30-sec rule: if no approach, circle and move. Come back at end.
  6. Error log. Every wrong Q → correct concept on a sticky. Day-before-exam revision = scan stickies only.

Exam-day non-negotiables (Physics paper)

  1. KCET has no negative marking. Attempt every single question, even guesses. Blank = 0; wrong = 0.
  2. First pass (40 min): Only questions you can solve in ≤60 sec. Skip long numerics and unfamiliar graphs.
  3. Second pass (30 min): Harder numerics — use units & options to eliminate. Most KCET options have one clear dimensional outlier.
  4. Last 10 min: Fill every OMR bubble. Any remaining blanks → eliminate 2 wrong options & guess.
  5. Watch sign conventions in lens, mirror, prism, EMI (Lenz direction) — 2–3 marks every year are lost here.
