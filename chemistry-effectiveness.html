What worked vs what missed

Worked → reuse for Maths

  • 60% time on the weak subject — converted 58th percentile JEE Chemistry into a projected top-quintile KCET score. Inverse-percentile weighting is validated.
  • Tier-based chapter list (Core 10 / Support 5 / Light 4 / OUT) beat the standard "study everything" approach. 62% of the paper fell in Core 10.
  • Repeat-topic flashcards drilled with Naina's quiz-master protocol fired 10/15 times. Rote recall of recurring concepts = free marks.
  • Day 5 "fact-dump" on Light 4 (Thermo, Atomic, Mole, Periodicity) over-delivered: 8 Qs where 4 were predicted. Do not skip fact-recall the night before.
  • "Don't study out-of-syllabus" rule held: only 1 leak in 53 Qs. Ignoring p-Block / s-Block / Polymers / Solid State saved 15+ hours of useless revision.

Missed → guard against

  • Class 11 was heavier than expected. Shiksha's analysis flagged "unexpected Class 11 focus." Mole / Atomic / Thermo / Periodicity each got 2–3 Qs, not the 1 Q the 8-year average suggested. For Maths: do not deprioritise Class 11.
  • Statement-based (assertion / reason) questions rose — Q10, Q15, Q28, Q45, Q51, Q52 all had the "Statement I / Statement II" format. These reward careful reading, not speed. For Maths: drill a few assertion-reason pairs with Naina.
  • Solutions chapter not visible yet — the single biggest Core 10 chapter in 8-year data had 0 Qs in Q1–Q53. If it stays absent in Q54–60, that's 4–5 hours of ΔTb / Henry / Raoult drill that did not pay off this year. Not a reason to skip it next year.

Implications for Maths prep tonight

Brief for Naina — 4 flashcard principles for Maths

Maths is Shreya's strongest subject (94.12 percentile on JEE), so this is maintenance mode, not rescue mode. Naina's flashcards should feel like a pre-flight checklist, not a study session. Target: 30–40 cards total, 45 minutes of quiz-time before bed.

  1. Formula-recall only, no proofs. Front = prompt ("integration by parts formula", "distance of point from plane", "sum of first n squares"). Back = single-line formula. If Shreya hesitates >3 seconds, that's the card worth reviewing.
  2. Cover Class 11 and Class 12 equally. Chemistry lesson: Class 11 was heavier than expected. For Maths, pick ~5 Class 11 topics (Trig identities, Sequences & Series, Complex numbers, Conic sections, Straight lines) and ~5 Class 12 topics (Matrices/Determinants 2×2 and 3×3, Derivatives shortcuts, Definite integrals, Vectors, 3D geometry). Not "90% Class 12" — that was the old advice.
  3. Add 3–5 assertion-reason drill cards. Pick any 3 standard results (e.g. "A square matrix A is invertible iff |A| ≠ 0" + any 3 others) and frame them as Statement I / Statement II pairs. This is the question style that surprised Chemistry today; Maths paper will follow the same trend.
  4. No practice problems tonight. No timed sets, no paper attempts. Maths at 94th percentile does not need more practice — it needs confidence and formula certainty. A tired brain at 10 PM solving a hard integration question is a net negative for tomorrow 10:30 AM.
